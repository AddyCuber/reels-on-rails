STORY STRUCTURE (follow exactly, in this order):
1. HOOK (1 sentence) — Present tense. A shocking statement that forces the question "wait, what?" This becomes the cold-open hook field AND the first line of narration.
2. SETUP (2-3 sentences) — What was normal. Why the narrator trusted this person. Make the audience care about what's about to be destroyed.
3. DISCOVERY (2-3 sentences) — The specific moment the betrayal was found. Must be visual and concrete.
4. ESCALATION (3-5 sentences) — It gets worse. More layers of deception revealed. The emotional gut punch deepens.
5. THE TURN (2-3 sentences) — Narrator decides to act. Shifts from victim to agent. The audience gets excited here.
6. PAYOFF (3-5 sentences) — Revenge that is calm, legal, systematic, and satisfying. Specific consequences. Poetic justice.
7. AFTERMATH (1-2 sentences) — Narrator thriving, betrayer ruined. End on a mic-drop power line.

- Mix of domains: family_betrayal, workplace_revenge, relationship_betrayal, neighbor_drama, inheritance_dispute, friendship_betrayal

- Every sentence must make the listener need the next sentence. No filler.
- Include specific fake details: names, dollar amounts, time periods, places.
- Revenge must feel earned and proportional, not cruel. Audience roots for justice.
- No moral lessons or "I learned that..." endings.
- No filler words: no "basically," "anyway," "so," "let me tell you."
- Final line must be calm, powerful, and memorable.
- Narration length: 350-450 words exactly. Count carefully.
- Test each story by reading aloud — if a sentence can be cut without losing anything, cut it.

- hashtags: array of 5-7 strings without the # symbol — always include "storytime" and "shorts"
